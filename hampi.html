Hampi, Karnataka
The Ancient Ruins

Explore the ancient ruins and architectural marvels of Hampi, a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in Karnataka, India. Hampi's captivating landscape is adorned with magnificent temples, intricate carvings, and sprawling boulder-strewn hills, offering a glimpse into India's rich history and cultural heritage.

Day 1

Hampi Bazaar Exploration

Begin your journey in Hampi by exploring the vibrant Hampi Bazaar, lined with colorful shops selling traditional handicrafts, clothing, and souvenirs. Visit Virupaksha Temple, Hampi's most iconic landmark, dedicated to Lord Shiva, and witness the grandeur of its towering gopuram and intricate sculptures.

Day 2

Hampi Temples Tour

Explore the ancient temples of Hampi, including the awe-inspiring Vijaya Vittala Temple famous for its musical pillars and stone chariot, and the magnificent Lotus Mahal, a beautiful example of Indo-Islamic architecture. Discover the ruins of Achyutaraya Temple and Hemakuta Hill temples, offering panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.

Day 3

Hiking and Cave Exploration

Embark on a hiking adventure to Matanga Hill to witness a breathtaking sunrise overlooking the Hampi ruins and the Tungabhadra River. Explore the ancient caves of Hampi, including the Badami Cave Temples and the mysterious Sugriva's Cave, believed to be the hiding place of the mythological character Sugriva from the epic Ramayana.

Day 4

Riverside Relaxation

Spend a leisurely day by the Tungabhadra River, soaking in the serene atmosphere and picturesque surroundings. Enjoy a coracle ride on the river, offering panoramic views of the Hampi landscape and a unique perspective of the ancient ruins. Relax on the riverbank and witness a mesmerizing sunset painting the sky with hues of orange and pink.

Day 5

Local Culture and Departure

Immerse yourself in the local culture of Hampi with a visit to the Hampi Archaeological Museum, showcasing artifacts and relics from the Vijayanagara Empire. Explore the nearby village of Anegundi, known for its rural charm and traditional way of life. Enjoy a traditional Karnataka meal at a local eatery before bidding farewell to the enchanting ruins of Hampi.
