Kashmir
Paradise on Earth

Discover the breathtaking beauty and cultural heritage of Kashmir, often referred to as "Paradise on Earth". With its snow-capped mountains, lush valleys, and serene lakes, Kashmir offers a mesmerizing experience for travelers seeking tranquility and adventure.

Day 1

Arrival and Valley Exploration

Arrive in Srinagar, the summer capital of Jammu and Kashmir, and explore the famous Mughal Gardens like Nishat Bagh and Shalimar Bagh. Enjoy a shikara ride on the serene Dal Lake, surrounded by snow-capped mountains and floating gardens. In the evening, stroll through the local markets and savor traditional Kashmiri cuisine.

Day 2

Gulmarg Excursion

Embark on a day trip to Gulmarg, known for its scenic beauty and adventure activities like skiing and snowboarding (in winter). Take the Gulmarg Gondola, one of the highest cable cars in the world, for breathtaking views of the snow-clad peaks. Enjoy a picnic amidst the lush meadows or indulge in golfing at the highest golf course in the world.

Day 3

Pahalgam Exploration

Drive to Pahalgam, a picturesque town known for its lush greenery and scenic beauty. Visit Betaab Valley, named after the Bollywood movie "Betaab", Aru Valley, and Chandanwari, surrounded by snow-capped mountains and gushing rivers. Explore the local markets for souvenirs and handicrafts.

Day 4

Srinagar City Tour

Explore Srinagar city and visit attractions like Shankaracharya Temple, Jamia Masjid, and Hazratbal Shrine. Take a leisurely walk around the vibrant markets of Lal Chowk, known for their handicrafts, spices, and dry fruits. In the evening, enjoy a traditional Kashmiri Wazwan dinner, featuring a variety of delectable dishes.

Day 5

Cultural Experience and Departure

Experience the rich cultural heritage of Kashmir with a visit to a traditional Kashmiri houseboat on Dal Lake. Witness a mesmerizing live demonstration of carpet weaving and papier-mâché art, showcasing Kashmir's centuries-old craftsmanship. Explore the local markets for souvenirs like Pashmina shawls, Kashmiri spices, and handcrafted wooden artifacts before bidding farewell to this enchanting land of lakes and mountains.
